# Why Humidity Is Everything for Crested Geckos

Crested geckos (*Correlophus ciliatus*) are native to the tropical forests of New Caledonia, where humidity regularly exceeds 70%. Replicating this in captivity is one of the most important aspects of their husbandry — see our full [Crested Gecko care guide](/guides/crested-gecko/) for the rest of their housing and diet needs.

## The Target Range: 60-80%

Aim for **60-80% relative humidity** throughout the enclosure. A brief drop to 50% during the day is acceptable. In fact, a "wet-dry cycle" (misting heavily at night, letting it dry during the day) mimics natural conditions and prevents respiratory issues.

## How to Achieve Proper Humidity

- Mist the enclosure once or twice daily, focusing on the walls and plants rather than directly on the gecko.
- Live or high-quality fake plants hold moisture and raise ambient humidity.
- Coconut fiber or a bio-active substrate retains moisture far better than paper towels.
- Screen tops release humidity quickly. If your enclosure struggles, cover 50-70% of the top with glass or acrylic.

## Monitoring Humidity

A **digital hygrometer** is non-negotiable. Analog gauges are notoriously inaccurate. Place the probe mid-enclosure, away from the misting point.

## Signs of Low Humidity

- Retained shed (especially on toes - can lead to constriction and loss of digits) — see our [complete guide to reptile shedding](/blog/reptile-shedding-complete-guide/) for how to handle it safely
- Wrinkled or sunken skin
- Reduced activity and appetite

Keep it moist, keep it right. Your crested gecko will thank you with years of flappy-footed company. For natural history, see the [Crested Gecko encyclopedia profile](/encyclopedia/animal/crested-gecko/), or browse the rest of our [Geckos care guide category](/guides/category/geckos/).
