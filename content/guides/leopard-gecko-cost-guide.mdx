# How Much Does a Leopard Gecko Really Cost?

<AffiliateDisclosure />

Leopard geckos are one of the more budget-friendly reptiles to keep, but "budget-friendly" still means a real setup, not a shortcut version. For the full care picture beyond budgeting, see our complete [Leopard Gecko care guide](/guides/leopard-gecko/).

## The Gecko Itself

A standard or wild-type leopard gecko from a pet store runs $20 to $45. Reputable breeders often charge $50 to $150 even for basic morphs, which sounds backwards until you factor in health guarantees and proper care history. Popular morphs land around $75 to $300, and rare designer morphs can run $500 to $3,500 or more. If you're buying online, add $35 to $100 for live shipping.

## Vet Costs to Plan For

An annual wellness exam runs $85 to $105. That's the routine cost. What catches people off guard is that sick or emergency visits run $100 to $800 or more, and if diagnostics or surgery get involved, it climbs from there. Our [Leopard Gecko health issues guide](/blog/leopard-gecko-health-issues-guide/) covers the conditions most likely to trigger one of those visits. Keeping a small emergency fund set aside is worth more peace of mind than it costs.

## The Bottom Line

Leopard geckos are genuinely one of the more affordable reptiles to keep long-term: low monthly costs, a long lifespan, and minimal equipment turnover once the initial setup is done. The upfront cost is where most of the money goes, and it's worth spending properly on the thermostat and enclosure size rather than cutting corners there.
