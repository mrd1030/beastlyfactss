# How Much Does a Guinea Pig Really Cost?

<AffiliateDisclosure />

Guinea pigs are cheap to adopt and expensive to underestimate. The animal itself costs almost nothing, but guinea pigs are social animals that need a same-species companion, so most of the real budgeting math here is really about caring for two, not one. For the full care picture beyond budgeting, see our complete [Guinea Pig care guide](/guides/guinea-pig/).

For a pair, that breaks down to roughly $30 to $50 in pellets, $20 to $50 in fresh vegetables, and $40 to $60 in <AffiliateLink href="https://amzn.to/4h9WW2u" product="Timothy hay (Oxbow Western Timothy Hay, 90 oz)">timothy hay</AffiliateLink> each month. <FunFact>
Guinea pigs were domesticated more than 3,000 years ago in the Andes, originally raised as livestock rather than pets. That deep history as a group-living animal is part of why they're so social today, exactly the trait that turns a single-guinea-pig budget into a two-guinea-pig one.

## Vet Costs

An annual wellness exam runs $50 to $100. Sick or emergency visits run $100 to $300 or more, and complex issues like GI stasis, covered in our [Guinea Pig health issues guide](/blog/guinea-pig-health-issues-guide/), can push well past that. Guinea pigs need a cavy-savvy exotic vet, not a general small-animal clinic, so it's worth finding one before you need one. No vaccines are required for this species.

## Why the Pair Matters for Your Budget

Guinea pigs kept alone tend to become withdrawn and depressed, so nearly every credible source recommends keeping them in pairs or small groups from the start. That effectively doubles your food and vet-visit costs compared to what a single-guinea-pig budget might suggest, but it's not really optional if you want a guinea pig that's actually thriving. Budget for two from day one rather than adding a second later and being surprised by the jump.

## The Real Budgeting Takeaway

The guinea pig itself is the cheap part. What actually determines your long-term cost is the size and quality of the cage and whether you're budgeting honestly for two animals instead of one from the start.
