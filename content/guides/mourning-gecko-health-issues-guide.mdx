# Common Mourning Gecko Health Issues (Egg-Laying Changes the Picture)

<VetDisclaimer />
<AffiliateDisclosure />

Mourning geckos are hardy in a properly humid, planted enclosure, but one biological fact shapes its health risks more than anything else: every animal is female, and every mature female lays eggs regularly, with or without a mate present. See our [Mourning Gecko tank setup guide](/blog/mourning-gecko-tank-setup-guide/) for the husbandry side of preventing most of this list, and our full [Mourning Gecko care guide](/guides/mourning-gecko/) for everything else.

## Metabolic Bone Disease (MBD)

The flagship health issue, driven by calcium or vitamin D3 deficiency, often combined with insufficient UVB. Watch for a kinked or wavy tail, depleted calcium sacs (the two white ovals under the throat), curved limbs, appetite loss, loss of coordination, and in severe cases, seizures.

<FunFact>
Mature female mourning geckos lay a clutch of two eggs roughly every 4 to 6 weeks, with or without a male present. That relentless egg-laying schedule is exactly why calcium demand runs so high in this species compared to most other beginner geckos, it's not an occasional breeding event, it's a constant baseline drain.
</FunFact>

## Egg-Binding (Dystocia)

Follows directly from the point above. A female that's calcium-depleted or dehydrated can fail to pass her eggs. This is a genuine emergency, not something to wait out at home, untreated dystocia is fatal.

## Retained Shed

Caused by low humidity. Shed that doesn't come off cleanly can constrict around the toes or tail tip. Generally manageable at home, <AffiliateLink href="https://amzn.to/4vPKMjX" product="Fine mist spray bottle">raise humidity and provide a moist hide</AffiliateLink>, but see a vet if tissue looks like it's dying or the constriction won't release.

## Mouth Rot and Parasites

Mouth rot appears as redness, swelling, or a cottage-cheese-like discharge around the mouth. Parasites cause weight loss and poor appetite despite normal feeding, diagnosed through a fecal exam. Both need a vet.

## A General Warning Sign

A visibly thin tail combined with weight loss usually points to an underlying issue, whether that's parasites, disease, or simply insufficient feeding for a colony that's grown larger than you originally planned for.

## The Husbandry Connection Worth Remembering

Skipping UVB or calcium and D3 supplementation doesn't just risk MBD in the abstract, it directly raises egg-binding risk in a species that's laying eggs constantly regardless of whether you're trying to breed it.
