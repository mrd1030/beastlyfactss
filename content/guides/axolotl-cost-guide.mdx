# How Much Does an Axolotl Really Cost?

<AffiliateDisclosure />

Axolotls look deceptively simple to keep, a tank of water and a strange, smiling salamander. The real cost driver isn't obvious until you get into the specifics: keeping the water cold enough is what actually determines your budget. For everything else about day-to-day care, see our full [Axolotl care guide](/guides/axolotl/).

## The Axolotl Itself: $30 to $100

Standard morphs run $30 to $100, with rarer colors like mosaic or GFP pushing the price higher still. This is actually one of the smaller pieces of the overall cost picture, the tank and temperature control matter a lot more.

Most keepers land around $200 to $500 for a basic setup, or $450 to $900 once a proper chiller and a fully cycled filter are factored in. The chiller is the line item that actually separates the low and high ends of that range, and it's worth understanding why before you decide to skip it, our [Axolotl tank setup guide](/blog/axolotl-tank-setup-guide/) covers the temperature requirement in full. <FunFact>
A chiller isn't an optional upgrade the way it might be for other tank setups, it's closer to a heater's opposite number. Most aquarium pets are kept warmer than room temperature, axolotls are one of the few common ones that need active cooling instead, which is exactly why this single item can double the price of an otherwise modest setup.
</FunFact>

## Ongoing Costs: $15 to $45 a Month

The chiller's electricity draw is the cost most first-time owners don't budget for, and it's the one that varies most by climate and season. If you live somewhere warm, running a chiller through summer months adds up in a way a simple filter-and-heater setup for most other aquatic pets wouldn't. Getting the water chemistry right also heads off a lot of the [health issues](/blog/axolotl-health-issues-guide/) that trace back to poor water quality in the first place.

## The Real Budgeting Lesson

Axolotls need cool water, ideally 60 to 68°F, and most homes run warmer than that for a meaningful part of the year. If you can't reliably keep the water in range without a chiller, that's not an optional upgrade, it's part of the actual cost of keeping this species responsibly.

Once the tank and temperature side is sorted, day-to-day care is fairly hands-off, this isn't a species you handle much anyway, more on that in our [Axolotl handling guide](/blog/axolotl-handling-guide/).
