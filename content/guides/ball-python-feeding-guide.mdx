# Ball Python Feeding: Everything You Need to Know

Ball pythons are one of the most popular pet snakes in the world, and one of the most notorious food refusers. Understanding their feeding behavior will save you a lot of stress.

## Feeding Schedule

- **Hatchlings (0-6 months):** Every 5-7 days
- **Juveniles (6-18 months):** Every 7-10 days  
- **Adults (18+ months):** Every 10-14 days

## Prey Size

Feed prey that is roughly the same width as the widest point of your snake's body. For most adult ball pythons, that's a medium rat. Too large = regurgitation; too small = nutritional inadequacy.

## Frozen/Thawed is Best

Always feed **frozen/thawed (F/T)** rodents. Live prey can injure your snake. Thaw in warm (not hot) water or a bag submerged in warm water. Never microwave. The prey should feel warm to the touch.

## Why Ball Pythons Refuse Food

1. **Breeding season (Oct-March):** Males especially go off food during this period - completely normal
2. **Shedding:** Most snakes refuse food when in shed (opaque eyes, dull skin)
3. **Incorrect temperatures/humidity:** The #1 husbandry-related cause
4. **Stress:** New enclosure, too much handling, too little hide cover
5. **Seasonal response:** Wild instinct to fast in cooler months

## When to Actually Worry

A healthy adult ball python can go 3-6 months without eating. Worry if: rapid weight loss, lethargy plus refusal, mucus around the mouth, or wheezing accompanies the fast.

Feed correctly, be patient, and trust the process.
