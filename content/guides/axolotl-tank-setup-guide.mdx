# Axolotl Tank Setup: Cold Water and No Gravel

<AffiliateDisclosure />

Axolotl husbandry comes down to a short list of rules that matter a lot: keep the water cold, skip the gravel, and don't overthink the rest. Get those two things right and this is a genuinely manageable pet. This covers the setup itself, for the cost of building it, see our [Axolotl cost guide](/blog/axolotl-cost-guide/), or the full [Axolotl care guide](/guides/axolotl/) for everything else.

## Tank Size

A <AffiliateLink href="https://amzn.to/4eSbKl2" product="20 gallon long tank">20-gallon long tank</AffiliateLink> is the minimum for one axolotl, with a 40-gallon breeder better if you're keeping two. Floor space matters more than height for this bottom-dwelling species. Use a secure lid, axolotls are capable of climbing out given the chance.

## Water Temperature: The Defining Requirement

Keep the water between 60 and 68°F. Above 72°F starts to cause chronic stress, and it can become fatal in the mid-70s. Do not use a heater. Instead, plan for an <AffiliateLink href="https://amzn.to/4wCcGzE" product="Aquarium chiller, 1/3 HP compressor (79 gal capacity)">aquarium chiller</AffiliateLink> (or the <AffiliateLink href="https://www.amazon.com/dp/B001EM1E8U?tag=beastlyfacts-20" product="JBJ Aquarium Arctica Titanium Chiller, 1/10 HP">JBJ Arctica Titanium</AffiliateLink> if you want a quieter premium option) or, in cooler climates, fan-based cooling to keep temperatures in range, especially through warm months. A <AffiliateLink href="https://amzn.to/3QHrM81" product="Digital thermometer/hygrometer combo">reliable thermometer</AffiliateLink> makes it easy to catch drift before it becomes a problem, the health consequences of getting this wrong are covered in full in our [Axolotl health issues guide](/blog/axolotl-health-issues-guide/).

## Water Parameters

Ammonia and nitrite should both read 0, nitrate should stay low, ideally under 10 ppm, and pH should sit around 7.4 to 7.8. Check these with a <AffiliateLink href="https://amzn.to/4p1T7yd" product="Water test kit">water test kit</AffiliateLink>. The tank needs to be fully cycled before an axolotl goes in, this isn't a species that tolerates an uncycled tank while it establishes itself.

<FunFact>
Axolotls never go through the land-based metamorphosis most salamanders do, they keep their gills and stay fully aquatic for their entire lives. That's exactly why nothing in a proper axolotl setup, unlike a lot of other amphibian tanks, needs a land area or a basking spot at all.
</FunFact>

## Substrate: The Other Non-Negotiable Rule

Use fine sand with grains under 1mm, or skip substrate entirely and go bare-bottom. Never use gravel or small rocks. Axolotls are opportunistic eaters and will ingest loose substrate along with their food, and anything larger than fine sand carries a real impaction risk, one of the health issues covered in our [Axolotl health issues guide](/blog/axolotl-health-issues-guide/). Keep juveniles under 5 inches on a bare-bottom tank until they're larger and less prone to accidental ingestion.

## Filtration

Use low flow. Axolotls dislike current and can become stressed by filters designed for more active fish. A gentle <AffiliateLink href="https://amzn.to/3T8xyAd" product="Sponge filter">sponge filter</AffiliateLink> or a canister filter with the output diffused through a spray bar both work well.

## Diet

Earthworms or nightcrawlers, cut into appropriate sizes for juveniles, are the nutritionally complete staple. <AffiliateLink href="https://amzn.to/4tR5M7U" product="Soft Sinking Pellets for Axolotls">Sinking pellets</AffiliateLink> (salmon-based or invertebrate-formulated options) work as a supplement, and bloodworms make a good occasional treat. Adults eat every 2 to 3 days, juveniles daily.

## A Few Other Details

Keep lighting dim, axolotls don't have eyelids and are sensitive to bright light. Provide hides for security. And keep the tank species-only, axolotls don't do well housed with fish, which can nip at their gills or outcompete them for food.

Once this setup is in place, day-to-day handling should stay minimal, see our [Axolotl handling guide](/blog/axolotl-handling-guide/) for when it's actually necessary, or browse the rest of our [Amphibians care guide category](/guides/category/amphibians/) and the [Axolotl encyclopedia profile](/encyclopedia/animal/axolotl/) for more.
