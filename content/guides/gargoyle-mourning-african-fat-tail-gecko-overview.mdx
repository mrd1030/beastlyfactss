# Three Geckos, Three Very Different Pets

"Gecko" sounds like one kind of animal, but these three barely share a care sheet. One needs to stay cool, one needs belly heat. One you'll never touch, one you'll hold daily. One digs, the other two climb. If you've been picturing all geckos as roughly the same pet, this trio is the clearest way to see how wrong that assumption is.

## Gargoyle Gecko: The One Where Heat Is the Enemy

Most reptile care revolves around keeping things warm enough. Gargoyle geckos flip that: room temperature is correct, and anything much above 82°F starts to cause real problems. They're calmer as adults than as skittish juveniles, and unlike their cousin the crested gecko, a gargoyle's bite can actually break skin, though its dropped tail, also unlike a crested gecko's, grows back.

## Mourning Gecko: The Pet You'll Never Hold

Every mourning gecko is female. They reproduce without a male at all, which is why they're commonly sold and kept as small self-multiplying colonies rather than single animals. They're also tiny, fast, and genuinely not a handling species, this is a display animal you watch through the glass of a lushly planted vivarium, not one you take out.

## African Fat-Tailed Gecko: The Digger

Close cousin of the leopard gecko and just as genuinely handleable, calm enough that it's often recommended alongside leos as a great starter reptile. The real difference is underfoot: fat-tails are burrowers, ground-dwelling and needing depth to dig into, plus more humidity and a moist hide that a leopard gecko doesn't require.
