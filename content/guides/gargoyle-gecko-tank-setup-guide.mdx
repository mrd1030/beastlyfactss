# Gargoyle Gecko Tank Setup: A Cool-Climate Species That Doesn't Want Your Heat Lamp

<AffiliateDisclosure />

Most reptile care is about adding warmth. Gargoyle gecko care is mostly about not overdoing it, this is a cool-climate species. For the full care picture beyond the enclosure, see our complete [Gargoyle Gecko care guide](/guides/gargoyle-gecko/).

## Enclosure Size

Minimum for a single adult is an <AffiliateLink href="https://amzn.to/4vlb6Bp" product="18x18x24 in glass terrarium (arboreal)">18x18x24 inch enclosure</AffiliateLink>, with 24x24x24 inches as the ideal. Vertical space matters more than floor space, this is an arboreal, climbing species. Hatchlings and juveniles under about 12 grams do better in a smaller grow-out enclosure temporarily. House one gecko per enclosure, they're territorial, a point covered in more detail in our [Gargoyle Gecko handling guide](/blog/gargoyle-gecko-handling-guide/).

## Temperature: The Defining Rule

Comfortable range is 72 to 78°F during the day, dropping into the low 70s or even mid-60s at night without issue. The hard limit: sustained temperatures above about 82°F cause real stress, and anything pushing into the upper 80s is dangerous.

Most homes need no dedicated heat source at all. If your home consistently runs below about 68°F, a low-wattage bulb or ceramic heat emitter on a thermostat is reasonable, but skip heat entirely if your room already sits in the low-to-mid 70s. This is one of the ways a gargoyle gecko setup actually costs less to run than a desert species, see our [Gargoyle Gecko cost guide](/blog/gargoyle-gecko-cost-guide/) for the full breakdown.

## Humidity: The Daily Cycle That Prevents Most Health Problems

Target 50 to 70%. Mist heavily in the evening, and let it dry back down toward 50% before misting again. Constant, unbroken moisture causes respiratory infection, one of the most common preventable problems in this species, covered in our [Gargoyle Gecko health issues guide](/blog/gargoyle-gecko-health-issues-guide/). A <AffiliateLink href="https://amzn.to/3QHrM81" product="Digital thermometer/hygrometer combo">thermometer and hygrometer combo</AffiliateLink> makes it easy to confirm the cycle is actually happening rather than guessing.

Gargoyle geckos mostly drink water droplets off leaves and walls rather than from a dish, though a shallow dish is still worth providing. A <AffiliateLink href="https://amzn.to/4vPKMjX" product="Fine mist spray bottle">fine mist spray bottle</AffiliateLink> is really all you need to run the daily cycle.

## Substrate

<AffiliateLink href="https://amzn.to/4wqEOFU" product="Bioactive substrate mix">Coconut fiber</AffiliateLink> (like Zoo Med Eco Earth) works well for humidity retention, and paper towel is a solid budget option, especially for hatchlings or a sick gecko. A bioactive soil mix with a drainage layer and a cleanup crew is another good option. Avoid loose, ingestible substrates, sand, wood chips, and gravel all carry a real impaction risk, discussed further in our [Gargoyle Gecko health issues guide](/blog/gargoyle-gecko-health-issues-guide/).

## Lighting

Gargoyle geckos can survive without UVB since a complete powdered diet supplies vitamin D3. Low-level <AffiliateLink href="https://www.amazon.com/dp/B00M9ONY1W?tag=beastlyfacts-20" product="Zoo Med 5.0 T5-HO 14&quot; Terrarium Hood with ReptiSun T5 HO 5.0 UVB Lamp">UVB</AffiliateLink>, a 12-inch low-output T5 bulb positioned for a basking-area UVI of roughly 1.0 to 2.0, offers real digestive and immune benefits. Run a 12-hour light cycle, with no light overnight. UVB doesn't pass through glass or plastic, so the bulb needs to sit directly over open mesh.

## What Makes a Gargoyle Setup Different

Heavy clutter, not sparse decor, is the right instinct here. <AffiliateLink href="https://amzn.to/3QQNV3L" product="Climbing branch (Mopani wood)">Branches, vines, and cork bark</AffiliateLink> genuinely help prevent floppy tail syndrome by giving your gecko somewhere other than bare glass to rest. Gargoyle geckos climb glass poorly compared to crested geckos, so grippable branches matter more here than in a typical crested gecko setup, a difference covered further in our [Gargoyle Gecko handling guide](/blog/gargoyle-gecko-handling-guide/).

Ventilation matters just as much as clutter: a screen top and real airflow let the enclosure dry out within about 24 hours after misting. And remember this is a temperature ceiling, not a floor, keep the enclosure away from sunny windows and other heat sources rather than trying to warm it up.

<FunFact>
Crested geckos stick to glass with ease. Gargoyle geckos, a close relative, are noticeably worse climbers on smooth surfaces and lean on branches and cork bark instead. It's a small anatomical difference with a real practical consequence: a sparsely decorated gargoyle enclosure just pushes the gecko toward sleeping flat against the glass, which is exactly what causes floppy tail syndrome.
</FunFact>
