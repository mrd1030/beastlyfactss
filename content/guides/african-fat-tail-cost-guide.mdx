# How Much Does an African Fat-Tailed Gecko Really Cost?

<AffiliateDisclosure />

African fat-tailed geckos cost more than most beginner geckos, and morphs push that number considerably higher. For the full care picture beyond budgeting, see our complete [African Fat-Tailed Gecko care guide](/guides/african-fat-tail/).

## The Animal Itself: $75 to $600, Averaging $100 to $200 for Normals

Normal, wild-type patterned animals are the cheapest, commonly around $100. Morphs change the price substantially: mid-range combinations like Amelanistic or single-gene Oreo animals often run $200 to $400, while rare or high-end combos, Whiteout, Zulu, or stacked multi-gene animals, can reach $600 to $1,000 or more, occasionally higher for truly rare lines.

## Ongoing Costs: Roughly $10 to $25 a Month

Gut-loaded crickets, dubia roaches, and occasional mealworms, plus <AffiliateLink href="https://www.amazon.com/dp/B0D7JWFV5W?tag=beastlyfacts-20" product="Repashy Calcium Plus 6 oz JAR">calcium and multivitamin supplements</AffiliateLink>. Electricity for a heat mat and optional UVB is modest, so most of the monthly spend is food.

<FunFact>
The "fat tail" isn't just a name, it's a real energy reserve. Like a leopard gecko, this species stores fat in its tail and can draw on it during lean periods, which is part of why a healthy fat-tail's tail should always look plump rather than thin.
</FunFact>

## Vet Costs

A routine exam commonly runs $35 to $105, plus a fecal test around $65 to $110. Illness or emergency visits with diagnostics typically run $150 to $300 or higher, with surgical intervention, for example egg-binding, costing more still. Our [African Fat-Tailed Gecko health issues guide](/blog/african-fat-tail-health-issues-guide/) covers the conditions most likely to trigger one of those visits.

## Lifespan: A Genuinely Long Commitment

Care sources commonly cite 15 to 20 years, and the best-documented figure comes from an academic longevity database (AnAge) showing a maximum recorded captive lifespan of just over 16 years. Either way, it's a long-term commitment, well beyond many other beginner reptiles, including the leopard gecko this species is constantly compared to.
