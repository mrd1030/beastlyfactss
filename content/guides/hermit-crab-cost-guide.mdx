# How Much Does a Hermit Crab Really Cost?

<AffiliateDisclosure />

Hermit crabs are often sold as an impulse buy at beach shops and boardwalk stands, which is exactly the wrong way to think about them. Done properly, they need a real setup, and they're social animals that shouldn't be kept alone. For everything else about day-to-day care, see our full [Hermit Crab care guide](/guides/hermit-crab/).

## The Crabs Themselves: $3 to $40 Each

Usually the smallest line item in the whole budget. Keep at least two, hermit crabs are social and don't do well in isolation, so budgeting for a single crab isn't really an option if you want to keep them properly.

Most complete setups land between $130 and $450, depending mostly on tank size and how many crabs you're starting with. That last line covers the extras that are easy to underestimate, including a pair of <AffiliateLink href="https://amzn.to/4eKyN0W" product="Large soak-able water dish">soakable water dishes</AffiliateLink>, since hermit crabs need both a fresh water dish and a separate marine saltwater dish, not just one. For the full walkthrough of what belongs in that setup, see our [Hermit Crab tank setup guide](/blog/hermit-crab-tank-setup-guide/).

<FunFact>
In the wild, hermit crabs looking to trade up will actually queue by size next to a vacant shell in what researchers call a vacancy chain, waiting their turn to try it on before the next crab in line gets a shot. It's part of why keeping several spare shells on hand, even in captivity, keeps a crab from settling for one that fits poorly.
</FunFact>

## Ongoing Costs: Roughly $10 to $30 a Month

Food and general supplies make up most of the monthly spend for hermit crabs. This is a genuinely low-cost pet to maintain once the initial setup is built, most of the expense is front-loaded into getting the tank right the first time.

## Why "Cheap Boardwalk Crab" Is a Misleading Way to Think About Cost

The low individual price of a hermit crab, often just a few dollars, creates the impression that this is a low-investment pet overall. In reality, the setup, tank, substrate depth, humidity control, and multiple spare shells, is what actually determines whether a hermit crab thrives, and none of that is optional. Skipping the real setup to save money is a common reason hermit crabs from boardwalk shops don't survive long, not because the species is inherently fragile, but because the cheap version of ownership genuinely isn't adequate care. That gap between minimal and adequate care shows up again in our [Hermit Crab health issues guide](/blog/hermit-crab-health-issues-guide/), where most problems trace straight back to husbandry shortcuts.

Hermit crabs can live 10 to 20 years or more when kept well, which makes the upfront investment in a proper setup a reasonable one relative to how long you'll actually have the animal.
