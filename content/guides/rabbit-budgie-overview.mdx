# Rabbit and Budgie: Two Classic Pets, Almost Always Kept Too Small

Both of these are among the most commonly kept pets outside of cats and dogs, and both are, more often than not, housed in something significantly smaller than they actually need. That's really the story that connects them.

## Rabbit: Bigger Space, Bigger Commitment Than the Hutch Suggests

The old-fashioned wire hutch that "rabbit cage" still calls to mind is considerably smaller than current welfare standards recommend. Rabbits also need a rabbit-savvy exotic vet, not a standard small-animal clinic, and a diet that's overwhelmingly hay, not the pellet-and-carrot combination most people picture.

## Budgie: Small Bird, Real Space Requirements

Budgies are often sold in cages barely bigger than the bird itself, when what they actually need is width to fly side to side, not just perch space. Add an avian vet (a genuinely specialized field) and a pellet-forward diet instead of the seed mix they're usually sold with, and budgie care looks pretty different from the starter-kit version.
