# 10 Surprising Humpback Whale Facts

Humpback whales are famous for their beautiful songs and dramatic breaches, but they have many other surprising behaviors and abilities. Here are ten facts that show why these gentle giants continue to amaze researchers and whale watchers.

1. Their songs can travel for miles. Male humpbacks sing complex, evolving songs that can be heard across entire ocean basins. The songs change every year and appear to spread through populations like cultural knowledge.

2. They hunt using bubble nets. Groups of humpbacks work together to blow bubbles in a circle, trapping fish inside. They then swim upward with mouths open to gulp huge amounts of prey at once. It’s impressive teamwork.

3. They migrate incredibly long distances. Many humpbacks travel over 5,000 miles each year between cold feeding waters and warm breeding areas. They make this journey while living mostly off stored body fat.

4. Every tail pattern is unique. The black and white markings on the underside of a humpback’s tail flukes are completely individual, like fingerprints. Researchers use these patterns to identify and track specific whales for decades.

5. They love to breach. Humpbacks often leap dramatically out of the water and crash back down. Scientists aren’t completely sure why, but possible reasons include communication, removing parasites, or simply play.

6. Calves drink enormous amounts of milk. A humpback calf can consume up to 150 gallons of very rich milk every day. This helps them grow at an incredible rate in their first months of life.

7. Their pectoral fins are massive. A humpback’s front flippers can be up to one-third of its total body length. They use them for steering, slapping the water, and herding fish during feeding.

8. They can be surprisingly playful with boats. Humpbacks sometimes approach vessels, spy-hop to look around, or gently interact with kayakers. While they should always be observed from a safe distance, they often seem curious.

9. They nearly went extinct. Heavy whaling in the 20th century brought humpback populations to the brink. Thanks to international protection, many populations have made a strong recovery in recent decades.

10. They can communicate across vast distances. Their calls and songs can travel hundreds or even thousands of miles underwater under the right conditions. It’s like having a natural long-distance communication system.
