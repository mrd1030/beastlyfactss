# 10 Surprising Madagascar Hissing Cockroach Facts

Say "cockroach" and most people picture something to avoid, not something to keep. Madagascar hissing cockroaches are a genuinely different animal, literally the loudest, cleanest, and most surprising member of the family. For care basics, see our full [Hissing Cockroach care guide](/guides/hissing-cockroach/).

1. They hiss through their abdomen, not their mouth. The sound comes from air forced rapidly through modified breathing holes (spiracles) on the fourth segment of their abdomen, a trick almost no other insect uses to make noise.

2. Their hiss can hit around 90 decibels and be heard from roughly 12 feet away, genuinely startling for an insect that's barely 2 to 3 inches long.

3. There isn't just one hiss. Researchers have identified four distinct types: a disturbance hiss when startled, a loud alarm hiss meant to scare off predators, and two separate hisses males use during courtship and combat with rival males.

4. Males do most of the hissing. They also carry raised "horns" behind the head, used to headbutt and wrestle rival males over territory and mates, something females don't do.

5. They give birth to live young. Most cockroach species lay a hardened egg case and abandon it, but hissing cockroaches keep the case inside the body until the nymphs are ready, then release them live, a strategy called ovoviviparity.

6. They live in stable, family-like colonies. Groups include overlapping generations living together with a recognizable social structure, rather than the solitary lives most insects lead.

7. They can't fly, and they're slow. Completely wingless and not built for speed, they're one of the more escape-proof insects anyone keeps as a pet.

8. Their exoskeleton is genuinely tough, dense enough that most small predators can't easily bite through it, part of why they've thrived on the forest floor of Madagascar for so long.

9. They're endemic to Madagascar and can't survive outside a warm, humid climate. In the wild, they live in and under rotting logs, helping break down decaying plant matter as part of the local ecosystem.

10. Despite the reputation cockroaches have, they're surprisingly clean animals. They groom themselves regularly, don't carry the disease-vector reputation of pest cockroach species, and need real tropical heat and humidity to survive, so an escapee has no shot at becoming a household infestation.

<FunFact>
That 90-decibel hiss is made entirely without vocal cords, since insects don't have any. It's pure airflow, forced through the same spiracles a cockroach normally just breathes through, repurposed into one of the loudest sounds any insect on Earth produces.
</FunFact>
