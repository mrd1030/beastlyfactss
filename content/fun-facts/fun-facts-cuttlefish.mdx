# 10 Surprising Cuttlefish Facts

Cuttlefish are often overshadowed by octopuses, but they are every bit as fascinating and in some ways even more impressive. With lightning-fast camouflage and strange abilities, these masters of disguise have plenty of surprises. Here are ten facts worth knowing.

1. They are masters of camouflage. Cuttlefish can change color, pattern, and skin texture in a fraction of a second to perfectly match their surroundings. Some can even make their skin look like moving sand or coral.

2. They have unique W-shaped pupils. Their eyes have a distinctive W-shaped pupil that helps them see well in varying light conditions and may improve depth perception and contrast detection.

3. They also have three hearts and blue blood. Like their octopus relatives, cuttlefish have three hearts and copper-based blue blood. Two hearts serve the gills while the third pumps blood to the rest of the body.

4. They can hypnotize prey with color. Some cuttlefish use pulsing color patterns to confuse or “hypnotize” prey like shrimp before striking. The moving waves of color hold the prey’s attention just long enough.

5. They have a built-in buoyancy device. Cuttlefish have an internal shell called a cuttlebone. By adjusting gas and liquid inside it, they can control their buoyancy and hover effortlessly in the water.

6. They are surprisingly intelligent. Cuttlefish can solve puzzles, remember solutions, and even show self-control in experiments by waiting for a better reward instead of taking a smaller one immediately.

7. Most live only 1-2 years. Like many cephalopods, cuttlefish have short but intense lives. They grow quickly, reproduce once, and die shortly afterward.

8. Males put on flashy displays. During mating season, males display bright, shifting color patterns to attract females and warn off rivals. Some smaller males even disguise themselves as females to sneak past bigger competitors.

9. Their ink serves multiple purposes. When threatened, cuttlefish release ink that can confuse predators. Some also release a “dummy” ink cloud that looks and moves like a cuttlefish, acting as a decoy while the real one escapes.

10. They can walk on their arms. Cuttlefish often use their arms to crawl along the seafloor in a walking motion. Combined with their camouflage abilities, this makes them incredibly stealthy hunters.
