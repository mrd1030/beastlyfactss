# 10 Surprising Golden Retriever Facts

<AffiliateDisclosure />

Golden retrievers are one of the most popular dog breeds for good reason, but there’s more to them than just a friendly face and wagging tail. Here are ten facts that often surprise even longtime golden owners.

1. They were originally bred to retrieve game from water. The breed was developed in Scotland in the 1800s specifically for retrieving shot ducks and other waterfowl. Their water-repellent coat and gentle mouth were carefully selected traits.

2. They have a famous “soft mouth.” Goldens can carry delicate items like eggs without breaking them. This gentle bite was essential for hunting dogs and remains one of their most endearing qualities today.

3. They are top service dog candidates. Golden retrievers are among the most common breeds used as guide dogs, therapy dogs, and assistance dogs thanks to their calm temperament, intelligence, and eagerness to please.

4. They are extremely food motivated. While this makes training easier in many cases, it also means goldens will happily eat things they shouldn’t. Counter surfing is a well-known golden talent.

5. They have a higher risk of certain cancers. Golden retrievers have one of the higher cancer rates among dog breeds. Regular vet checkups and awareness are important for owners of this breed.

6. They are natural swimmers. Thanks to their history as water retrievers, most goldens love swimming and take to water very naturally. Their webbed feet and strong build help them move efficiently.

8. They make excellent therapy dogs. Their gentle and patient nature makes goldens especially good at visiting hospitals, schools, and nursing homes where their calm presence helps reduce stress.

9. The breed has Scottish origins. Lord Tweedmouth in Scotland developed the foundation of the modern golden retriever by crossing flat-coated retrievers with other breeds, including the now-extinct Tweed Water Spaniel.
