# Research Brief Template — Species Deep-Dive Batch

Use this before writing a species' cost / handling / health-issues / tank-setup
deep-dive batch (see `GUIDE_TEMPLATE.mdx` for the article shape itself). Run
this brief once per species, in whatever research tool you're using outside
Claude Code, then hand the results back to build the four articles.

Copy everything below the line and replace `[Species Name]`.

---

I'm writing four articles about [Species Name] as a pet for beastlyfacts.com: a cost breakdown, a handling/temperament guide, a common health issues guide, and a tank/enclosure setup guide. I need real, verifiable information for each, not generic filler. Please research the following and flag anything you're not confident is accurate rather than guessing.

**1. Cost breakdown**
- Realistic purchase price range for the animal itself from reputable breeders/retailers (not just one listing, a real range), and whether morphs/color variants meaningfully change price
- Upfront setup cost: enclosure, heating, lighting, substrate, hides/decor, thermometer/hygrometer — itemized if possible, with a total range
- Monthly ongoing cost: food, supplements, electricity for heating/lighting
- Typical vet costs: routine checkup range and what a common emergency visit might run
- Lifespan, since that matters for total cost-of-ownership framing

**2. Handling and temperament**
- How this species actually behaves when handled: skittish, docile, prone to biting/dropping tail/other defense mechanisms, etc.
- Any species-specific handling risks or things a first-time keeper would get wrong (e.g., grip technique, how it reacts to being restrained, whether it tolerates daily handling or is more look-don't-touch)
- How this compares to closely related/similarly-marketed species, if there's a common point of confusion worth addressing

**3. Common health issues**
- The 3-5 most common health problems specific to this species (not generic reptile/pet advice), with real symptoms and what typically causes each (diet, humidity, temperature, genetics, etc.)
- Which of these are emergencies vs. manageable-at-home vs. "always see a vet"
- Any species-specific husbandry mistake that directly causes a specific health issue (this is usually the most useful angle)

**4. Tank/enclosure setup**
- Minimum and ideal enclosure size (for a single adult)
- Temperature range/gradient and whether it needs a heat source at all
- Humidity requirements and how they're typically achieved
- Substrate recommendations (and any substrate to avoid, with why)
- Lighting needs (UVB or not, photoperiod)
- Any non-obvious enclosure requirement specific to this species (climbing space, burrowing depth, hiding spots, etc.)

For all of the above: cite where a number or claim comes from where possible (breeder site, care sheet, vet resource), and explicitly say if something is a commonly-repeated claim you couldn't independently verify. I'd rather have "here's a range, sourced from X and Y" than an unsourced single number.
